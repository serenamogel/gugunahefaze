Amanda Lemos Biography

Born on May 22, 1987, Lemos is known to have begun her professional mixed martial arts journey in the Brazilian regional circuit, notably competing in Jungle Fight. With an undefeated record of 5–0, she contended for the Jungle Fight Women’s Bantamweight Championship against Mayra Cantuária at Jungle Fight 85 on January 23, 2016. Their first encounter resulted in a majority draw, leading to a rematch at Jungle Fight 88 on June 25, 2016, where Lemos claimed the title via third-round knockout.

Lemos made her UFC debut on short notice, replacing Lina Länsberg, against Leslie Smith at UFC Fight Night: Nelson vs. Ponzinibbio on July 16, 2017, where she suffered a second-round knockout loss. Following this, she faced a suspension from USADA for two years due to testing positive for stanozolol and its metabolite in November 2017.

After serving her suspension, Lemos returned to the octagon in the strawweight division. Notable victories include a technical submission win against Miranda Granger at UFC Fight Night 165 and unanimous decision victories over Mizuki Inoue at UFC on ESPN: Munhoz vs. Edgar and Lívia Renata Souza at UFC 259.

Lemos continued her success with a technical knockout victory over Montserrat Ruiz at UFC on ESPN 26 and a split decision win against Angela Hill at UFC Fight Night: Lewis vs. Daukaus, earning the Fight of the Night bonus.

Although Lemos faced a setback with a loss to Jéssica Andrade at UFC Fight Night 205, she bounced back with a guillotine choke submission win over Michelle Waterson at UFC on ABC 3, earning her first Performance of the Night bonus. She also secured a technical knockout win against Marina Rodriguez at UFC Fight Night 214.

Lemos challenged Zhang Weili for the UFC Women’s Strawweight Championship at UFC 292 on August 19, 2023, but fell short, losing via unanimous decision. Despite this, she returned to the win column with a unanimous decision victory over Mackenzie Dern at UFC 298, earning another Fight of the Night award.
